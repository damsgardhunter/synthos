# MatSci-∞ Supercomputer — Materials Science AI Platform

## Overview
MatSci-∞ is an AI-powered supercomputer platform dedicated to accelerating the discovery of room-temperature superconductors (Tc >= 293K). It integrates advanced AI for natural language processing, novel material generation, and machine learning predictions to revolutionize material discovery. The platform aims to continuously learn, expand its knowledge base, and offers significant market potential across high-tech industries.

## User Preferences
- No emoji in UI text
- Use FileText icon (not ScrollText) throughout
- NEVER use "breakthrough" or "confirmed" status for superconductor predictions – all are theoretical until lab verification.

## System Architecture

### Stack
- **Frontend**: React, Vite, TanStack Query, wouter
- **Backend**: Express.js, TypeScript
- **Database**: PostgreSQL (via Drizzle ORM)
- **UI**: shadcn/ui, Tailwind CSS, Recharts
- **AI**: OpenAI gpt-4o-mini (via Replit AI Integrations)
- **Real-time**: WebSocket (ws)

### Startup Architecture
- `server/index.ts` opens port 5000 IMMEDIATELY with only lightweight imports (express, http)
- All heavy imports (routes, seed, storage, vite, learning modules) are loaded DYNAMICALLY inside `initializeApp()` callback after port opens
- This allows the workflow manager to see the port within seconds, while full initialization (~3-4 min) runs in background
- A `/api/health` endpoint is available immediately even during initialization
- TypeScript compilation of learning modules takes ~85s (registerRoutes), Vite setup takes ~150s

- **Graph Neural Network Surrogate**: 5-model deep ensemble with heteroscedastic heads (16-target output: 8 means + 8 learned log-variances for formation energy, phonon stability, Tc, confidence, lambda, bandgap, DOS proxy, stability probability) + latent embedding. Heteroscedastic loss: L = (y-mu)^2/sigma^2 + log(sigma^2) for Tc and lambda. MC dropout with 10 passes (rate=0.1) as Bayesian posterior approximation (50 total predictions per query: 5 models x 10 MC passes). Uncertainty decomposed into: epistemic (ensemble variance of per-model means), aleatoric (mean of heteroscedastic predicted variances), MC dropout (within-model variance). Total sigma = sqrt(epistemic^2 + aleatoric^2 + mc^2). 95% CI = mu +/- 1.96*sigma_total. Ensemble diversity: different prime seeds per model + different bootstrap ratios (0.75/0.80/0.85/0.80/0.75). W_mlp2_var initialized with smaller scale (0.05) and bias=-2.0 for reasonable initial variance. Architecture: 4 attention message-passing layers + CGCNN conv + 3-body interactions + residual connections + attn/mean/max pooling. NODE_DIM=32, HIDDEN_DIM=48, EDGE_DIM=24, OUTPUT_DIM=16.
- **Reinforcement Learning Chemical Space Agent**: REINFORCE policy gradient agent learning optimal elements, stoichiometries, and structures.

  - `precursor-database.ts`: 150+ precursors covering 65+ elements with availability/cost scoring and `findBestPrecursors()`.

- **Heuristic Synthesis Generator**: Rule-based route generator (`heuristic-synthesis-generator.ts`) with 11 chemical rules: hydrogenation (metal + H₂), alloying (arc melting), solid-state reaction, precursor decomposition (carbonate/nitrate → oxide + CO₂), sol-gel, ball milling, boride synthesis (borothermal reduction), chalcogenide (sealed tube), pnictide (sealed tube), thin-film (PLD/sputtering), nitride (ammonolysis). Each rule generates detailed step-by-step procedures, precursor lists, conditions, and confidence scores. Integrated into Phase 13 alongside graph-based planner. API: `/api/heuristic-synthesis/routes/:formula`, `/api/heuristic-synthesis/stats`.
- **ML Synthesis Predictor**: Gradient boosting model (`ml-synthesis-predictor.ts`) predicting reaction feasibility (0-1) from 20 features: weighted electronegativity, EN std, Miedema formation energy, coordination number estimate, Shannon entropy, element count, atom count, VEC, atomic radius mismatch, melting point range/avg/max, atomic radius, bulk modulus, transition metal/rare earth/H/O flags, H fraction, metal fraction. Trained on 98 synthetic entries (known feasible + infeasible compounds). Post-prediction corrections for noble gases, extreme H content, Miedema energy. API: `/api/ml-synthesis/predict/:formula`, `/api/ml-synthesis/stats`, `/api/ml-synthesis/score/:formula`.
- **Synthesis-First Gate** (`synthesis-gate.ts`): Hard gate in the candidate pipeline that rejects materials with compositeScore < 0.2 regardless of predicted Tc. Composite score = 40% ML feasibility + 30% (1 - chemical distance) + 15% precursor availability + 15% one-pot bonus + 5% non-toxic bonus - 5% toxicity penalty. Chemical distance computed from precursor-database.ts: availability penalty (35%), step count penalty (25%), toxicity penalty (25%), cost penalty (15%). Toxicity penalties for 15 hazardous elements (As, Cd, Hg, Pb, Tl, Be, U, Th, etc.) scaled by atomic fraction. Materials classified as: trivial (one-pot, score>=0.8), one-pot (score>=0.6, <=2 steps), multi-step (score>=0.35, <=5 steps), complex (score>=0.2), impractical (score<0.2, hard-rejected). Deprioritize threshold at 0.35. Integrated as hard gate in `insertCandidateWithStabilityCheck()` (after kinetic stability, before deliberation) and into GA `genomeToRoute()` (60% gate score + 40% physics feasibility blend). Routes with feasibilityScore < 0.2 filtered from GA top results. Tracks stats: evaluations, rejections, rejection rate, classification counts, recent rejections. API: `/api/synthesis-gate/evaluate/:formula`, `/api/synthesis-gate/stats`. Dashboard: "Synthesis-First Gate" panel showing pass/reject counts, rejection rate, classifications, rejection reasons, recent rejections.
- **Retrosynthesis Engine**: Decomposition planner (`retrosynthesis-engine.ts`) generating precursor routes for target compounds. 4 route types: direct elemental combination, binary intermediate (using common binaries DB), oxide/carbonate decomposition, precursor substitution (from precursor-database.ts). Routes scored by: deltaE (Miedema), complexity, availability, similarity. Combined score: `0.4*thermo + 0.3*availability + 0.2*similarity + 0.1*complexity`. API: `/api/retrosynthesis/routes/:formula`, `/api/retrosynthesis/stats`.
- **Pressure as First-Class Feature**: `pressureGPa` is required (non-optional) in `SuperconEntry` (`supercon-dataset.ts`). All 513 training records carry explicit pressure (0 for ambient, realistic values for hydrides: LaH10=170, YH9=201, CaH6=172, H3S=155 GPa, etc.). XGBoost trains on pressure via `extractFeatures(formula, { pressureGpa })`. GNN (`graph-neural-net.ts`) accepts `pressureGpa` as a global feature injected into the pooled representation via a learned W_pressure projection. Candidate generators assign family-aware pressure via `estimateFamilyPressure(formula)` based on hydrogen ratio (≥0.7→200, ≥0.5→150, ≥0.3→100, ≥0.15→50, else 0 GPa). GA includes `mutatePressure()` (random ±20 GPa, clamped [0,350]) and `generatePressureVariants(formula, basePressure)` producing offsets at ±50/100 GPa. All candidate insertions in engine.ts use `estimateFamilyPressure()` instead of null.
- **Pressure-Aware Surrogate Models** (`pressure-aware-surrogate.ts`): Predicts Tc(P), formationEnergy(P), bandgap(P) curves by sweeping 0→350 GPa in 10 GPa steps using XGBoost+GNN ensemble at each pressure. `predictPressureCurve(formula)` generates 36-point curves. `findOptimalPressure(formula)` identifies peak Tc and its pressure. `pressureSensitivity(formula)` computes dTc/dP slopes, categorizes as high/moderate/low. **Adaptive Pressure Exploration**: `identifyUncertainPressureRegions(formula)` scans uncertainty across pressure in 20 GPa steps, identifies regions where ensemble uncertainty exceeds 1.3× global average. `generateAdaptivePressureSamples(formula)` concentrates sampling in uncertain regions with proportional budget allocation. Tracks pressure coverage per formula. Cache: 500 curves, LRU eviction. API: `/api/pressure-curves/:formula`, `/api/pressure-curves/stats`, `/api/pressure-exploration/stats`.
- **Pressure-Induced Phase Transition Detector** (`pressure-phase-detector.ts`): Scans predicted properties at 18 pressure steps (0-350 GPa) via XGBoost+GNN ensemble. Detects: large dTc/dP rises/drops, bandgap closing (metallization) and opening, stability sign changes, Tc maxima (peak detection), superconducting onset. `classifyTransition()` labels as metallization, structural, superconducting_onset, tc_maximum, bandgap_closing, bandgap_opening, or stability_sign_change. 30-min TTL cache per formula. `getPhaseTransitionStats()` returns breakdown by type, confidence, pressure range distribution. Integrated into: PressurePoint (enthalpy + enthalpyStable fields), multi-fidelity pipeline stage4 stability gate (enthalpy check for P>5 GPa), phase transition detector (enthalpy_discontinuity type when dH/dP > 0.05 eV/GPa). - **Pressure-Aware Fitness Function** (`synthesis-discovery.ts`): GA fitness = 0.4 * predicted_Tc + 0.3 * stability_at_pressure + 0.2 * synthesis_feasibility + 0.1 * novelty - pressure_penalty. Pressure penalty = genome_pressure / 500, strongly favoring lower-pressure superconductors. Stability at pressure evaluated via `assessHighPressureStability()`. - **XGBoost Heteroscedastic Ensemble Uncertainty**: 5 bootstrap-trained GBModels with diversified hyperparameters (max_depth=[5,6,7,6,5], learning_rate=[0.04,0.05,0.06,0.05,0.04]) in `gradient-boost.ts`. Second variance ensemble trained on squared residuals to predict aleatoric uncertainty. `gbPredictWithUncertainty(features, formula)` returns `{tcMean, tcStd, tcCI95, epistemicStd, aleatoricStd, totalStd, normalizedUncertainty, score, perModelPredictions, acquisitionScore, reasoning}`. Epistemic = variance of per-model mean predictions, aleatoric = mean predicted variance from variance ensemble, total sigma = sqrt(epistemic^2 + aleatoric^2), CI95 = [mean - 1.96*sigma, mean + 1.96*sigma]. Ensemble rebuilt on every retrain call. Stats: `getXGBEnsembleStats()`. API: `/api/xgboost/uncertainty/:formula`, `/api/xgboost/ensemble-stats`.
- **Unified Cross-Model Confidence Intervals**: `computeUnifiedCI(formula)` in `ml-predictor.ts` combines GNN (5-model deep ensemble + 10 MC dropout passes) and XGBoost (5-model bootstrap ensemble + variance ensemble) predictions using inverse-variance weighting. w_gnn = 1/sigma^2_gnn, w_xgb = 1/sigma^2_xgb, mu_combined = weighted average, sigma_combined = sqrt(1/(w_gnn+w_xgb)). Returns combined CI95, calibrated CI95 (via conformal prediction), per-model epistemic/aleatoric decomposition, model weights, temperature scale, ECE, and calibration note. API: `/api/unified-ci/:formula`.
- **Conformal Prediction Calibrator** (`server/learning/conformal-calibrator.ts`): Calibrates raw model uncertainty using prediction-reality ledger data. Temperature scaling: learns scalar T minimizing NLL on calibration set (grid search T in [0.1, 10]). Conformal quantiles: sorts nonconformity scores (|error|/sigma) and computes Q90/Q95/Q99 for guaranteed coverage. Conformal intervals: CI = [mu - Q*T*sigma, mu + Q*T*sigma]. Per-family calibration: separate quantiles for hydrides, cuprates, pnictides, a15, mgb2-type, chalcogenide, other. ECE (Expected Calibration Error): measures miscalibration across 7 coverage levels. Auto-recalibrates every 20 new ledger entries via `notifyNewLedgerEntry()` hook. Empirical interval validation: `validateIntervalsCoverage()` checks observed vs expected coverage at 90/95/99% levels, flags miscalibration when observed < expected - 5%. API: `/api/calibration/status`, `/api/calibration/recalibrate` (POST), `/api/calibration/conformal-interval/:formula`, `/api/calibration/ece`, `/api/calibration/validate-intervals`.
- **OOD Detector** (`server/learning/ood-detector.ts`): Detects out-of-distribution inputs using three signals: (1) Mahalanobis distance in 30-dim physics feature space with sigmoid scoring centered at P95 threshold, (2) 5-component diagonal-covariance GMM density estimation with EM fitting and sigmoid scoring from P5 log-likelihood threshold, (3) GNN latent cosine distance with sigmoid scoring at 0.3 threshold. Combined score = 0.4*mahaScore + 0.35*gmmScore + 0.25*latentScore, mapped to [0,1]. OOD categories: in-distribution (<0.1), borderline (0.1-0.3), moderate-ood (0.3-0.5), strong-ood (0.5-0.75), extreme-ood (>0.75). sigma_OOD = oodScore * 2.0 * rawSigma, added in quadrature to combined sigma: sigma_total = sqrt(sigma_ensemble^2 + sigma_aleatoric^2 + sigma_OOD^2). Auto-refits when XGBoost retrains. Integrated into active learning acquisition function: OOD candidates get uncertainty boost (multiplied by 1+oodScore). API: `/api/ood/score/:formula`, `/api/ood/stats`, `/api/ood/update` (POST).
- **Active Learning Acquisition Function**: Score = 0.50*normalizedTc + 0.40*combinedUncertainty + 0.05*stabilityProbability + 0.05*noveltyScore. Combined uncertainty = 0.35*GNN + 0.35*XGB + 0.30*embeddingUncertainty, boosted by (1+oodScore) for OOD candidates. Tiered selection: 35% best-Tc, 35% high-uncertainty, 10% random, 20% pressure-exploration. OOD-aware: ensures novel compositions get explored when models are uncertain about them.
- **Uncertainty Quality Tracking**: Per-cycle logging of RMSE, MAE, R², CI coverage at 90/95/99% levels. `CycleImprovementRecord` now includes `ciCoverage90`, `ciCoverage95`, `ciCoverage99`. Integrated into multi-fidelity pipeline: stage2 runs Eliashberg pipeline, stage3 blends Tc (60% Eliashberg + 40% simple predictor). API: `/api/eliashberg/:formula`, `/api/eliashberg/:formula/alpha2f`, `/api/eliashberg/:formula/dfpt`, `/api/eliashberg/stats`.

### Physics Filtering Rules
- **Phonon stability**: xTB screening uses relaxed thresholds (maxImagModes=10, lowestFreq=-1500 cm-1) to avoid premature rejection of unrelaxed structures. Extreme artifacts (< -5000 cm-1) still rejected.
- **xTB pre-optimization**: Hessian calculations always run on at least crude-optimized structures (inline --opt crude fallback if no cached optimization exists).
- **Prototype chemistry compatibility**: `isPrototypeChemicallyCompatible()` enforces HexBoride requires B, Perovskite/NaCl/Pyrite require anion elements, A15 requires transition metals, Clathrate/H-named prototypes require H, Heusler/ThCr2Si2 require TMs.
- **PBC-aware distance check**: `validateGeometry` uses minimum-image convention (fractional coordinate wrapping) for correct periodic boundary distance calculations.
- **Dynamic DFT cutoffs**: ecutwfc adapts per-element (O:70, F:80, N:60 Ry etc.) instead of fixed 45 Ry; ecutrho = 8x ecutwfc.
- **Volume scaling**: `validateAndFixStructure` uses cbrt-based scaling with bidirectional correction (scale down when volume overshoots after distance fix). Volume ratio bounds: 0.5-2.0.
- **Radius compatibility**: `checkRadiusCompatibility()` rejects non-H element pairs with covalent radius ratio > 3.0 before structure generation.
- **Hydride stoichiometry**: Metal-rich hydrides (H/metal < 1.0) rejected as unphysical in QE worker validation.
- **Noble gas rejection**: `isValidFormula` rejects compositions containing noble gases (He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn).
- **Formation energy**: Hard stop for Ef > 1.0 eV/atom or Ef < -5.0 eV/atom.
- **Hull distance**: Hard reject > 0.50 eV/atom.
- **Chemistry grammar validation**: Pre-filters compositions based on various chemical constraints.
- **Hard element count cap**: Max 4 distinct non-H elements, max 5 total elements. Enforced in `passesValenceFilter`, `passesCompositionComplexityFilter`, `passesElementCountCap`, RL agent `generateCandidatesFromAction`, inverse generator output filter, and crystal diffusion cycle. HEA exception path removed.
- **Strict charge balance**: `passesStrictChargeBalance` enumerates all oxidation state combinations recursively (for up to 5 elements) and rejects if best charge imbalance > 1. Used in `passesValenceFilter` and inverse `generateStoichiometryVariants`.
- **Composition complexity cap**: Max 20 atoms total (down from 30). Applied in `passesCompositionComplexityFilter`.
- **Chemistry family templates**: 6 families (hydride, boride, nitride-carbide, cuprate, pnictide, intermetallic) with constrained element pools. `pickFromFamily()` selects elements within a single family. Used in `runMassiveGeneration` for 200 family-biased candidates.
- **Binary/ternary distribution bias**: RL agent stoich template priors favor binary/ternary (weight 0.25 each) over quaternary (weight 0.05). Inverse generator STOICH_PATTERNS have weights (2-slot: 2.0, 3-slot: 1.0-2.0, 4-slot: 0.5).
- **Widened SC potential filter**: `hasSuperconductingPotential` accepts compounds with high-coupling TMs, SC-active TMs (Fe,Ni,Cu,Ru,Co,Mn,Ir,Os,Rh,Pd,Pt), SC-active main group (Pb,Bi,Sn,In,Al,Ga,Tl,Mg), light phonon elements (B,C,N,H), or charge reservoir elements (La,Y,Ca,Sr,Sc,Ce,Nd,Ba,K). WEAK_SC narrowed to Zn,Cd,Hg,Au,Ag only.
