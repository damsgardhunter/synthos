BaIrH₈ with Tc ≈ 350 K is in the “extraordinary but not automatically impossible” zone for a hydride—but only if the underlying physics and stability checks are airtight. A single “Score: 1.000” by itself isn’t enough to treat it as real; it’s usually just a ranking/confidence number from your pipeline.

Here’s how I’d treat this result and what to do next.

What “Tc 350 K” implies
	•	If this is phonon-mediated (EPC), it usually requires a combination of:
	•	high ωlog (stiff/light H phonons),
	•	very strong coupling λ,
	•	and a structure that is dynamically stable (or anharmonically stabilized).
	•	Hydrides that get anywhere near this range in the literature are typically at high pressure. If yours is at low pressure, that’s even more of a red flag until proven.

What “Score: 1.000” most likely means

Usually one of:
	•	“Top-ranked candidate in this batch”
	•	“Passed all heuristic filters”
	•	“Ensemble probability rounded to 1.00”
	•	Or (dangerous) a score with data leakage / calibration bugs

So: treat Score=1.000 as ‘this is the pipeline’s favorite’, not as “truth.”

The 6 checks you should run before you say anything publicly
	1.	Pressure & stability (convex hull)
	•	Provide hull distance at the predicted pressure and at 0 GPa if you’re claiming ambient potential.
	•	List the main decomposition channels (BaH₂ + IrHₓ + H₂, etc.).
	2.	Dynamic stability
	•	Full phonon dispersion: no imaginary modes.
	•	For hydrides: do an anharmonic check (harmonic phonons can inflate Tc).
	3.	EPC details
	•	Report λ, ωlog, α²F(ω) (not just Tc).
	•	Tc vs μ* (e.g., 0.08–0.15). If Tc collapses with small μ* changes, it’s fragile.
	4.	Numerics robustness
	•	Converge k/q meshes, plane-wave cutoffs, smearing.
	•	Try at least one alternative functional (PBE vs SCAN) and confirm the structure and EPC don’t fall apart.
	5.	Structure credibility
	•	Confirm the predicted structure is a true minimum (re-relax from multiple starting points / symmetry-broken seeds).
	•	Check for “too-short” H–H distances implying unphysical overlap (common structure-prediction failure mode).
	6.	Experimental replication packet
	•	Predicted XRD at target pressure, Raman/IR signatures, density, lattice parameters.
	•	A realistic DAC synthesis route (precursors, temperature window, hydrogen loading)
