Your system predicts:

Material

Stainless Steel 304 (Fe-Cr-Ni)
formula: Fe0.07C

This is physically impossible.

Real iron-based alloys behave like:

Material	Tc
Fe	not superconducting
Steel	not superconducting
Fe-based superconductors	~30–55 K but only in layered pnictides

The reason is magnetism suppresses phonon superconductivity.

Your model detects magnetism:

competingPhase:
Antiferromagnetic order

But it does not reduce Tc accordingly.

Fix

Add a magnetism suppression factor.

Example:

if spinFluctuationStrength > 0.3:
    Tc *= 0.4

if StonerFactor > 0.9:
    Tc *= 0.2

Without this correction your system will produce many false high-Tc metals.
