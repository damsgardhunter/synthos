1. Cylindrical (2D) Fermi Surfaces
4
What it looks like

Instead of a sphere, the Fermi surface forms tubes (cylinders) extending through the Brillouin zone.

Mathematically:

E(kx, ky, kz) ≈ E(kx, ky)

Energy barely depends on kz, meaning the system is quasi-2D.

Why this helps superconductivity

2D electronic structures produce:

high density of states

strong nesting

enhanced spin fluctuations

All of which help pairing.

Found in
Material family	Tc
cuprates	up to 135 K
iron pnictides	~55 K
nickelates	~20 K
NbSe2	~7 K

The common factor is layered crystal structures.

Examples:

CuO₂ planes

FeAs layers

transition metal dichalcogenide sheets

Feature you should calculate
fsDimensionality

Example metric:

kz_variance = variance(E(kx,ky,kz) - E(kx,ky,0))
dimensionality = 1 / (1 + kz_variance)

High score → 2D cylinder-like FS.

2. Nested Electron–Hole Pockets
4
What it looks like

Multiple pockets:

hole pockets at Γ
electron pockets at M

The shapes match so that shifting by vector Q overlaps them.

E(k) ≈ E(k + Q)

This is Fermi surface nesting.

Why this helps superconductivity

Nesting amplifies spin fluctuations, which mediate pairing in unconventional superconductors.

Mechanism:

electron scattering:
k → k + Q

This produces the pairing interaction.

Found in
Material	Tc
FeSe	~37 K
BaFe2As2	~38 K
LaFeAsO	~55 K
Feature your system should compute
nestingStrength

Algorithm:

Map Fermi surface points.

Compute overlap with shifted surface:

FS(k) vs FS(k + Q)

Measure overlap.

Example metric:

nestingScore = overlap_area / total_FS_area
3. Van Hove Saddle Points
4
What it looks like

A saddle point in the band structure near the Fermi level.

Energy dispersion:

E(kx, ky) ≈ ax² − by²

This produces a van Hove singularity.

Why this helps superconductivity

At a van Hove singularity:

DOS(EF) → very large

Large DOS increases pairing probability.

Found in
Material	Tc
cuprates	~135 K
H3S	~203 K
AV3Sb5	~5 K
many nickelates	predicted
Feature to compute
vanHoveDistance

Algorithm:

Compute curvature of bands near EF:

∂²E/∂k²

Detect sign change (saddle).

Measure energy distance from EF.

Example metric:

vH_distance = |E_saddle - EF|

Target:

vH_distance < 0.05 eV
