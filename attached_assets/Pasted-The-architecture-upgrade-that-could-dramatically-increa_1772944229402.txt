The architecture upgrade that could dramatically increase discovery rate in a system like yours is something I would call:

Closed-Loop Multi-Fidelity Scientific Discovery (CLMF)

Most materials discovery systems today — even advanced ones — still follow this pattern:

Generator
   ↓
Filter
   ↓
Surrogate prediction
   ↓
Occasional DFT

Even systems used by groups like DeepMind or datasets from Materials Project are still largely single-fidelity pipelines.

The real world of materials discovery is multi-scale physics:

chemistry → structure → phonons → electrons → superconductivity

Your platform already has pieces of this, but the architecture upgrade is to fully close the loop across multiple fidelities and physics layers.

The Key Idea

Instead of:

AI → surrogate → DFT

You run progressive physics resolution.

AI Generator
      ↓
Cheap Physics Filters
      ↓
Intermediate Physics
      ↓
High-Fidelity DFT
      ↓
Physics Feedback to Generator

This dramatically increases signal per expensive computation.

The Architecture
Layer 1 — Chemistry Generator

Inputs:

element priors

oxidation states

structural prototypes

RL exploration

Output:

candidate structure

Example:

YH6 clathrate
MgB2-type structure
LaH10 cage

Your prototype generator will plug in here.

Layer 2 — Ultrafast Physics Filters (milliseconds)

These filters reject 90–95% of bad candidates instantly.

Examples:

Radius mismatch filter
|rA − rB| < tolerance
Oxidation neutrality
Σ oxidation states = 0
Packing density
0.3 < packing_fraction < 0.75
Electron count rules

Many superconductors follow:

2–4 valence electrons per atom

This stage reduces compute by orders of magnitude.

Layer 3 — Semi-Empirical Physics (milliseconds–seconds)

Examples:

xTB relaxation

tight-binding energy

approximate band structure

Tools often used:

xTB

DFTB+

Outputs:

relaxed structure
approximate electronic features
Layer 4 — Surrogate Physics Models

Your system already has this.

Predict:

Tc
formation energy
electron-phonon coupling
density of states

These models should also produce uncertainty.

Candidates with high uncertainty go to DFT.

Layer 5 — Intermediate DFT (cheap settings)

Run quick calculations using:

Quantum ESPRESSO

Settings:

low k-mesh
low cutoff
fast SCF

Outputs:

formation energy
DOS
phonons (rough)
Layer 6 — Full DFT Physics

Only the best candidates reach here.

Compute:

full phonon spectrum
electron-phonon coupling
Eliashberg Tc

This stage is expensive but very accurate.

The Critical Missing Piece

The real innovation is the feedback engine.

Instead of just storing results, the system learns physics patterns.

Example learning signals:

structure → stable phonons
structure → unstable phonons
structure → strong EPC
structure → weak EPC

The generator then adapts.

How This Changes Discovery

Traditional search:

1 success per ~10,000 candidates

Closed-loop multi-fidelity:

1 success per ~300–800 candidates

This is 10–30× more efficient.
