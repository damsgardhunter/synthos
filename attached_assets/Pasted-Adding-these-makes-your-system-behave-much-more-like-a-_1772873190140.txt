1️⃣ Fermi Surface Nesting Constraint
4
Why this matters

Many high-Tc superconductors rely on strong Fermi surface nesting.

Examples:

Material	Role
Iron pnictides	nesting between electron + hole pockets
Cuprates	quasi-2D nested bands
Charge density wave superconductors	nesting drives pairing

Good nesting increases:

electron scattering
spin fluctuations
pairing interaction
How to compute it

Compute the nesting function

ξ(q) = Σ_k δ(ε(k)) δ(ε(k+q))

High ξ(q) means many states connectable by momentum q.

Inverse constraint

Your optimizer should enforce:

maximize nesting score
prefer quasi-2D Fermi surfaces
prefer multiple Fermi pockets
Implementation

Add after band structure calculation.

function computeFermiSurfaceNesting(bandStructure){
   return nestingScore
}

Then constrain:

if nestingScore < threshold:
    reject material
2️⃣ Orbital Hybridization Constraint
4
Why this matters

High Tc often requires specific orbital mixing.

Examples:

Material	Hybridization
Cuprates	Cu d + O p
MgB₂	B pσ bands
Iron pnictides	Fe d orbitals

This hybridization:

increases electron mobility
improves electron-phonon coupling
creates favorable band structures
Implementation

From DFT projection:

Projected Density of States (PDOS)

Compute orbital mixing ratio:

hybridization = overlap(d_orbitals, p_orbitals)

Constraint:

if hybridization < threshold:
    reject
3️⃣ Lifshitz Transition Proximity
4
Why it matters

Many superconductors sit near a Lifshitz transition.

This is when:

a new Fermi surface pocket appears

Near this point:

density of states spikes
pairing strength increases
Tc rises

Examples:

Material	Behavior
FeSe	Tc enhanced near Lifshitz
H₃S	pressure induced Lifshitz
Cuprates	doping driven
Implementation

From band structure:

distance = |band_edge - fermi_level|

Constraint:

if distance > 0.1 eV:
    penalize

Goal:

band edge near Fermi level
4️⃣ Quantum Critical Fluctuation Constraint
4
Why this matters

Many unconventional superconductors occur near quantum critical points.

Examples:

Material	QCP
Cuprates	doping critical point
Heavy fermions	magnetic QCP
Iron pnictides	magnetic instability

Near QCP:

spin fluctuations increase
pairing interaction strengthens
Implementation

Proxy indicators:

large spin susceptibility
nearly magnetic state
small magnetic energy difference

Constraint:

|E_nonmagnetic − E_magnetic| < threshold
5️⃣ Electronic Dimensionality Constraint
4
Why this matters

Many high-Tc superconductors are quasi-2D.

Examples:

Material	Structure
Cuprates	layered
Fe pnictides	layered
MgB₂	quasi 2D

2D systems have:

stronger correlations
better nesting
higher pairing probability
Implementation

Compute band dispersion anisotropy.

anisotropy = bandwidth_xy / bandwidth_z

Constraint:

anisotropy > 5
6️⃣ Phonon Soft Mode Constraint
4
Why it matters

Soft phonon modes can enhance electron-phonon coupling.

Seen in:

Material	Behavior
BaTiO₃	soft mode
H₃S	strong phonon softening
Pb	phonon anomaly

Soft modes mean:

lattice strongly interacts with electrons
Implementation

From phonon dispersion:

ω_min

Constraint:

0 < ω_min < small threshold

Reject if:

ω_min < 0

because that means instability.

7️⃣ Charge Transfer Energy Constraint
4
Why this matters

Cuprates are charge transfer superconductors.

Key parameter:

Δ = E_d − E_p

If Δ is optimal:

electrons move easily between orbitals
pairing increases
Implementation

From PDOS:

delta = d_band_center − p_band_center

Constraint:

0 eV < Δ < 3 eV

dielectric_constant ε
